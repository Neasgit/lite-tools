01 — Brand

Icon Concepts.

Seven directions for the channel icon. Each rendered at 64 / 32 / 16 px with a verdict. Approved direction lives in Brand Reference ·05.

07 Concepts 03 Sizes 02 Top picks
Geometric / Structural
A / 07

Filled Corners

Solid amber triangles at each corner. Evolution of the Viewfinder · same concept, much heavier visual weight. Reads as a framing device or evidence marker.

Strong pick
64px
32px
16px
Notes

Much more impact than line-only corners. Amber fills are bold at every size. Pattern stays readable at 16px.

B / 07

Crosshair

Target/scope sight. References bodycam and tactical footage. Circle plus cross · clean and geometric.

Strong concept
64px
32px
16px
Notes

Reads clearly at all sizes. Circle + cross is recognizable even at 16px. Feels tactical and documentary.

C / 07

Circle Emblem

Thin amber ring with bold amber initial inside. Expedition / documentary badge aesthetic · timeless and legible.

Clean & versatile
64px
32px
16px
Notes

Expedition badge feel. Works as a profile pic, watermark, favicon. The circle frames the letter without feeling like "just a monogram."

Footage / Recording
D / 07

Record

Large amber recording dot with thin outer ring. References bodycam HUD / REC indicator. No ambiguity about what this channel does.

High concept, generic shape
64px
32px
16px
Notes

Amber dot reads instantly as "recording." But a filled circle is the most generic possible shape · only works if the brand colour is well-known.

E / 07

Play Frame

Play triangle (amber) framed by thin corner marks. Combines the footage reference with the surveillance frame.

Good combo
64px
32px
16px
Notes

Play triangle dominates at every size. Corners are supporting detail · disappear at 16px but the triangle alone is the mark.

F / 07

Open Brackets

Just [ and ] in amber. Nothing inside · the empty space is the content "left in." Most conceptually pure interpretation of the channel name.

Conceptually strong, visually sparse
64px
32px
16px
Notes

Empty brackets = the thing left in, unseen. But two vertical strokes at 16px reads as "||" · loses the concept at small sizes.

Hybrid
G / 07

Square Crosshair

The Viewfinder's corner marks plus crosshair lines meeting at a centre dot. Square geometry throughout · bodycam HUD or tactical sight.

Best of both
64px
32px
16px
Notes

Same corner geometry as the wordmark. Crosshair lines add a targeting/HUD layer without a circle. Centre dot anchors it. Cohesive with the full brand.

Strongest picks

A · Filled Corners evolves the Viewfinder wordmark directly. Same language, more weight. B · Crosshair is more tactical. Both hold at 16px.

The hybrid

G · Square Crosshair combines both: corner marks plus crosshair lines in pure square geometry. Bridges Viewfinder and Crosshair. Cohesive with the wordmark, can feel busy at small sizes.

Set aside

E · Play Frame feels generic unless paired with corner marks. C, D, F are reference points, not live options.
